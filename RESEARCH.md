## Executive Summary

The dominant binary model of life and death — alive vs. dead, conscious vs. unconscious — is increasingly untenable in light of three converging lines of evidence:

1. **Neural death surges** demonstrate that the dying brain does not simply "turn off" but undergoes a final, highly organized burst of gamma-band coherence, suggesting a transient hyperconscious state at the boundary of biological viability (Borjigin et al., 2013; Xu et al., 2023).

2. **Post-mortem cellular recovery** via perfusion technologies (OrganEx, Yale 2022) shows that cellular death is not instantaneous but proceeds through a recoverable gradient — raising the question of what constitutes the *minimum viable consciousness* threshold.

3. **Integrated Information Theory (IIT)** provides a mathematical framework (Φ, the integrated information measure) that quantifies consciousness as a continuous variable, not a binary state — enabling, for the first time, a computable definition of "aliveness" that spans molecular, neural, and cognitive scales.

This document presents the **Consciousness Continuity Framework** — a synthesis of these research programs into a single computational model, anchored by the **Consciousness Continuity Integral (C_cont)**, the **Human Brain Zero (HB₀)** concept, and the **Infotropy Principle**.

## Part I: The Layered Architecture of Aliveness

Aliveness is not a single property but a hierarchical stack of nested information-processing systems. We define six layers, ordered from the most fundamental (quantum coherence) to the most emergent (social consciousness):

### The 6-Layer Hierarchy

| Layer | Name | Scale | Key Marker | Measurement |
|:-----:|:-----|:------|:-----------|:------------|
| **L1** | Quantum Biological Coherence | Subatomic–Molecular | Long-lived quantum coherence in biological chromophores | 2D electronic spectroscopy; quantum process tomography |
| **L2** | Cellular Metabolism & Molecular Identity | Cell | Active metabolic flux; oncRNA barcode expression | Metabolomics; single-cell RNA-seq; oncRNA panel (Arc Institute) |
| **L3** | Neural Oscillatory Coherence | Circuit | Gamma-band (30–100 Hz) synchrony across cortical columns | EEG/ECoG power spectral density; phase-amplitude coupling |
| **L4** | Integrated Sensory Experience | Network | Φ > 0 (non-zero integrated information) | IIT Φ-computation; perturbational complexity index (PCI) |
| **L5** | Self-Reflective Awareness | Whole-brain | Metacognitive access; autobiographical self-model | Higher-order thought paradigms; confidence ratings; default mode network integrity |
| **L6** | Social / Collective Consciousness | Inter-organism | Shared intentionality; cultural transmission | Hyperscanning (dual-EEG); behavioral synchrony metrics |

### Layer Dependencies

Each layer depends on the integrity of all layers below it. The collapse of a lower layer propagates upward:

```
L6  Social Consciousness         ← requires L5
L5  Self-Reflection              ← requires L4
L4  Integrated Experience (Φ>0)  ← requires L3
L3  Neural Coherence (γ-band)    ← requires L2
L2  Cellular Metabolism (oncRNA)  ← requires L1
L1  Quantum Coherence            ← fundamental
```

**Key insight:** Death is not a point — it is the sequential collapse of layers from L6 downward, with each layer exhibiting its own characteristic decay time. The "death surge" observed by Borjigin et al. represents a paradoxical *transient re-emergence* of L3–L4 coherence during the collapse of L2.

## Part II: The Three Pillars

The framework rests on three empirical research programs, each addressing a different scale of the aliveness hierarchy.

### Pillar 1: Molecular — oncRNA Barcodes

**Source:** Arc Institute, *Cell Reports Medicine* (2026)

Oncogenic RNA (oncRNA) species function as molecular barcodes of cellular identity and viability. Each cell type expresses a characteristic oncRNA signature that:

- Persists during normal cellular function (L2 integrity marker)
- Degrades in a predictable, cell-type-specific pattern during ischemia
- Can be detected in liquid biopsy (circulating oncRNA fragments)

**Relevance to consciousness:** oncRNA signatures provide a *molecular ground truth* for L2 integrity. If L2 has collapsed (oncRNA barcode absent), L3–L6 cannot be sustained regardless of external stimulation.

**Key parameters:**
- `M(t)` — Metabolic viability function derived from oncRNA panel
- `τ_M` — Cell-type-specific oncRNA half-life under ischemic conditions
- `M_threshold` — Minimum oncRNA signature for L2 viability

### Pillar 2: Neural — Human Brain Zero (HB₀) & Death Surges

**Sources:** Borjigin et al., *PNAS* (2013); Xu et al., *PNAS* (2023); OrganEx, Yale (2022)

#### The Death Surge Phenomenon

During cardiac arrest in both rats and humans, the brain exhibits a paradoxical surge in organized neural activity:

- **Gamma oscillations** (30–100 Hz) increase by 5–8× baseline in specific cortical regions
- **Directed connectivity** (anterior→posterior gamma coupling) transiently exceeds waking levels
- The surge occurs 30–120 seconds after cardiac arrest onset
- In humans, the surge correlates with prior seizure history and is observed in 2 of 4 patients studied (Xu et al., 2023)

#### HB₀ — Human Brain Zero

HB₀ defines the **minimum viable consciousness** — the smallest neural configuration that sustains integrated information (Φ > 0). Analogous to:

- **Absolute zero** in thermodynamics (unreachable but definable)
- **Ground state** in quantum mechanics (lowest energy configuration with non-zero properties)

HB₀ is characterized by:

```
HB₀ = {N_min, C_min, Φ_min}

where:
  N_min = minimum neuron count for Φ > 0
  C_min = minimum connectivity density
  Φ_min = smallest measurable integrated information
```

**Clinical significance:** HB₀ defines the boundary below which recovery of consciousness is physically impossible, regardless of technological intervention. OrganEx demonstrates that *cellular* recovery (L2) can occur well below HB₀ — but consciousness (L4+) cannot.

### Pillar 3: Theoretical — Integrated Information Theory (IIT)

**Sources:** Koch/BIAL Foundation (2026); Tononi (2004, 2008, 2015)

IIT postulates that consciousness is identical to integrated information (Φ) — a mathematical quantity measuring how much a system's whole exceeds the sum of its parts.

#### The 5D Φ-Vector

Standard IIT computes a scalar Φ. We extend this to a **5-dimensional Φ-vector** corresponding to the five measurable layers (L2–L6):

```
Φ⃗ = (Φ_molecular, Φ_neural, Φ_sensory, Φ_reflective, Φ_social)
```

Each component measures integrated information at its respective scale:

| Component | Layer | Measurement Proxy |
|:----------|:------|:-----------------|
| Φ_molecular | L2 | Mutual information in oncRNA co-expression networks |
| Φ_neural | L3 | Cross-frequency coupling in gamma-band EEG |
| Φ_sensory | L4 | Perturbational Complexity Index (PCI_st) |
| Φ_reflective | L5 | Metacognitive sensitivity (d') in confidence paradigms |
| Φ_social | L6 | Inter-brain synchrony in hyperscanning |

#### K(Φ) — The Complexity Metric

To collapse the 5D Φ-vector into a single usable metric, we define:

```
         5
K(Φ) =  Σ  wᵢ · Φᵢ · log₂(Φᵢ + 1)
        i=1

where:
  wᵢ = empirically derived weight for layer i
  Φᵢ = integrated information at layer i
```

The `log₂(Φᵢ + 1)` term ensures:
- K(Φ) = 0 when all Φᵢ = 0 (total information death)
- Diminishing returns at high Φ (biological saturation)
- Natural bits scaling

## Part III: The Unified Framework

### The Consciousness Continuity Integral

The central equation of the framework integrates all three pillars into a single measure of *total consciousness experienced over time*:

```
         ∞
C_cont = ∫  Φ(t) · S(t) · M(t) · R(t)  dt
         0

where:
  Φ(t) = Integrated Information (from IIT Φ-vector, scalar or K(Φ))
  S(t) = Neural Synchrony function (gamma coherence index, 0 ≤ S ≤ 1)
  M(t) = Metabolic Viability function (oncRNA-derived, 0 ≤ M ≤ 1)
  R(t) = Recoverability function (OrganEx-derived, 0 ≤ R ≤ 1)
```

**Interpretation:**
- `C_cont` represents the total "conscious experience content" accumulated over a lifetime
- The integrand goes to zero when *any* factor reaches zero — reflecting the layer dependency structure
- The product form captures the idea that consciousness requires *simultaneous* integrity across all scales

|:----------|:------|:---------------|
| Normal waking | Φ > 0, S ≈ 0.7, M ≈ 1.0, R = 1.0 | High |

### Cancer, Aging, and Death as Φ-Collapse

The framework reinterprets three major biological phenomena as variations of **Φ-collapse** — the progressive or sudden loss of integrated information:

#### Cancer as Local Φ-Divergence

Cancer cells exhibit *local* hyper-integration (oncRNA overexpression, autonomous signaling) while *global* integration degrades. In the Φ-vector:

```
Cancer: Φ_molecular ↑↑ (local), Φ_neural ↓ (if CNS), Φ_sensory ↓ (downstream)
```

Cancer is not a loss of aliveness but a **phase separation** — a region of the organism that maximizes its own Φ at the expense of the system's integrated Φ.

#### Aging as Gradual Φ-Erosion

Aging corresponds to the slow, monotonic decrease in K(Φ) driven primarily by:
- L1: Accumulating oxidative damage to quantum-coherent biological structures
- L2: Declining oncRNA fidelity; epigenetic drift
- L3: Reduced gamma-band power and connectivity

```
Aging: dK(Φ)/dt < 0, with acceleration after critical threshold
```

#### Death as Cascading Φ-Collapse

Death is the rapid, sequential collapse of Φ-vector components from L6 → L1, with the death surge representing a paradoxical transient *increase* in Φ_neural during the L3 collapse phase.

```
Death timeline:
  t₀:     Cardiac arrest
  t₀+0s:  Φ_social → 0 (L6 collapse)
  t₀+15s: Φ_reflective → 0 (L5 collapse)
  t₀+30s: Φ_neural ↑↑ SURGE (L3 paradox)
  t₀+2m:  Φ_neural → 0 (L3 collapse)
  t₀+5m:  Φ_sensory → 0 (L4 collapse)
```

## Part VI: The Infotropy Principle

### Statement

> **Infotropy** (portmanteau of *information* + *entropy*) is the measure of meaningful information loss in a conscious system. It is the time-derivative of consciousness destruction.

Formally:

```
           dC_cont
I(t) = − ────────
             dt

     = − d/dt [ Φ(t) · S(t) · M(t) · R(t) ]
```

When `I(t) > 0`, the system is *losing* conscious information (dying).
When `I(t) < 0`, the system is *gaining* conscious information (developing, learning, recovering).
When `I(t) = 0`, the system is in **conscious equilibrium**.

### The Death Surge as Negative Infotropy

The death surge represents a brief period of **negative infotropy** — `I(t) < 0` — during which the dying brain *gains* integrated information before final collapse. This is thermodynamically analogous to **latent heat** in a first-order phase transition: the system absorbs/releases energy at the phase boundary.

```
Normal waking:  I(t) ≈ 0     (equilibrium)
Cardiac arrest: I(t) >> 0    (rapid information loss)
Death surge:    I(t) < 0      (paradoxical information gain)
Final death:    I(t) → +∞    (complete collapse)
Post-mortem:    I(t) = 0      (no information to lose)
```

### Implications

1. **The death surge is not noise** — it is a thermodynamically necessary consequence of consciousness undergoing a first-order phase transition.

2. **Consciousness has latent information** — analogous to latent heat, there exists a quantity of "bound" integrated information that is released during the transition from conscious to non-conscious states.

3. **Recovery interventions should target I(t)** — the goal of consciousness-preserving medicine is to maintain `I(t) ≤ 0` (prevent infotropy from going positive).

4. **Infotropy is measurable** — given calibrated Φ(t), S(t), M(t), and R(t) functions, infotropy can be computed in real-time from clinical data streams.

### Infotropy and the Second Law

The Infotropy Principle is consistent with the Second Law of Thermodynamics: total entropy of the closed system (brain + environment) always increases. However, *locally* — within the conscious system — infotropy can temporarily decrease (negative I(t)) at the cost of increased entropy in the surrounding substrate (metabolic waste heat, ATP depletion).

The death surge is the consciousness analog of a **Maxwell's demon** operating at the phase boundary — briefly concentrating information before the substrate collapses.
